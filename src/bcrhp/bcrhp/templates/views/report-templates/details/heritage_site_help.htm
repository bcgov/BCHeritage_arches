Details Card Help Text

Images:
Use the left < and right > arrows to view the images.
Site Name:
Includes names associated with the historic place.
Heritage Site Location:
Provides the civic address(es) or location description for the historic place.
Site ID Number:
The Borden System number assigned when the record was created. The Borden System is a Canada-wide map index system to uniquely identify heritage and archaeological sites.
Official Recognition Information:
Describes the status of the historic place on the Register and its associated Registry types.
Registered:
The heritage site is officially recognized by a legislative enactment from the province or a municipality/regional district.
Federal Jurisdiction:
The authority for the site is the federal government.
Official Recognition Details:
Lists all government enactments of heritage status for the historic place.
Recognition Type:
Identifies the level of recognition (municipal, provincial, federal) and its type. Hover over or click the type for a definition
Recognition Government:
The authority responsible for applying the recognition.
Recognition Date:
The date the recognition was enacted.
Reference Number:
The number referencing the Bylaw, Order in Council, Council Resolution, etc. of the heritage enactment.
Chronology:
Lists Construction and other Significant Dates
Architect/Builders:
Lists any architect or builder associated with the historic place.
Statement of Significance:
Explains the heritage value of a historic place and describes what the recognition applies to, why the place is important or significant, and what elements contribute to the heritage value and must be maintained to preserve the value.
Heritage Class:
Defines the number and type of resources included in the historic place as well as its ownership classification.
Heritage Function:
Identifies the historic or current function or purpose of a historic place.
Heritage Theme:
